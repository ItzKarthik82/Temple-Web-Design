Festival Calendar

Hindu Festivals & Events

Celebrate sacred moments throughout the year

Major Annual Festivals

Celebrating the richness of Hindu tradition

Feb 26

Maha Shivaratri

The Great Night of Shiva - all-night vigil with fasting, chanting, and abhishekam every 3 hours.

Mar 14

Holi

Festival of Colors celebrating spring, love, and the victory of good over evil with vibrant colors.

Apr 6

Ugadi

Telugu and Kannada New Year with special prayers, traditional foods, and cultural programs.

Apr 17

Sri Rama Navami

Birth celebration of Lord Rama with Ramayana recitation, bhajans, and grand procession.

Aug 16

Janmashtami

Krishna's birthday celebrated with midnight abhishekam, bhajans, and Dahi Handi festivities.

Sep 7

Ganesh Chaturthi

Lord Ganesha's birthday with special pujas, cultural programs, and traditional offerings.

Oct 2-10

Navaratri

Nine nights honoring Goddess Durga with daily pujas, Garba/Dandiya dances, and cultural events.

Oct 12

Vijayadashami

Victory of good over evil - Durga worship culmination and Ayudha Puja for tools and vehicles.

Oct 20

Karva Chauth

Married women's fasting day for husband's wellbeing with moon worship and traditional rituals.

Nov 1

Diwali

Festival of Lights with Lakshmi puja, oil lamps, fireworks, and community celebration of light over darkness.

Nov 15

Karthika Masam

Sacred month dedicated to Lord Vishnu and Shiva with special daily prayers and observances.

Dec 25

Vaikunta Ekadashi

Most important Ekadashi for Vishnu devotees with fasting, all-night vigil, and special archana.
